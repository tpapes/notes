Enshittification

A term coined by Cory Doctorow to describe the process of online platforms making the internet less attractive and useful for users. (virtually all of this card is taken from his talk at DEF CON 31).

As he describes it, platforms start by catering to their users with a service that has some benefit compared to their competitors. Discord beat Teamspeak because of price and ease of use, Facebook beat MySpace because people thought Rupert Murdoch was draconican (Zuck really switched up on that, huh), and Uber beat taxi services because of low price points and how much easier it is to use an app than to talk to someone on the phone (ðŸ¤®). This is good business practice. It just makes sense that a better product would (and should) beat a worse one.

The problem comes with the fact that the platforms are often losing money to make their product more convenient than the other options (less ads + lower price + extra features = less revenue & higher costs). So, even though they're getting tons of users, things need to change if they want to keep the lights on.

This is when platforms pivot their attention to businesses.
