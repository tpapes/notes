Enshittification

A term coined by Cory Doctorow to describe how the merging of online platforms makes the internet less attractive and useful for users. (virtually all of this card is taken from his talk at DEF CON 31).

As he describes it, platforms start by catering to their users with a service that has some benefit compared to their competitors. This is good business practice, is great for consumers, and it just makes sense that a better product would (and should) beat a worse one.

The problem comes with the fact that the platforms are often losing money to make their product more convenient than the other options. So, even though they're getting tons of users, things need to change if they want to keep the lights on.

This is when platforms pivot their attention to businesses.
